## Revenue layers

1. Personal-use STL products establish discovery.
2. Collection bundles increase customer value.
3. Premium editions add detail, formats, and presentation.
4. Commercial licenses serve small-batch makers.
5. Physical editions test non-digital demand.
6. Membership drops reward repeat supporters.

## Cost model

Track design time, prototype resin, machine time, failed prints, rendering, copy, platform fees, support, tax assumptions, and maintenance. These are inputs, not universal constants. Profit claims remain assumptions until actual costs and realized revenue are recorded.

## Portfolio rule

Every product belongs to one collection and should strengthen at least one bundle path. Products with weak collection or customer value require revision even if their standalone aesthetics are appealing.
