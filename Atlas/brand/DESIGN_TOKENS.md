## Token rules

Tokens are product-specific declarations within a shared language. Numeric print dimensions are hypotheses until prototype-tested.

- **Corner radius:** broad and calm relative to object scale.
- **Wall thickness:** declared in millimetres and validated for resin, scale, hollowing, and load.
- **Chamfer style:** shallow, functional, and used to catch controlled highlights.
- **Fillet style:** continuous and generous at handled or load-transition edges.
- **Edge language:** few edge families per object; no accidental sharpness.
- **Texture density:** low; texture supports tactility without masking geometry.
- **Surface finish:** matte mineral, satin ceramic, or restrained stone simulation.
- **Relief depth:** shallow to moderate and support-aware.
- **Pattern scale:** architectural rhythm visible at thumbnail and physical scale.
- **Shadow language:** long soft gradients with a limited number of decisive cuts.
- **Visual weight:** grounded, not bulky.
- **Design balance:** asymmetric only when stability and calm remain clear.
- **Material simulation:** honest render cues; no implication that resin is natural stone.
